THE STORY

I moved to Australia in 1998.

I worked and learned how to say "crikey," and eventually got my citizenship. I stayed in Australia for ten years before moving back to Austin (my hometown) in 2008.

In 2004, I bought the old Falcon in Brisbane, where we lived. This was my first ever muscle car, something I'd wanted desperately since my teenage years.

This car was not an investment project, it was the realization of my lifelong desire to own a badass muscle car.

I knew it needed a lot of work. It was fun to drive as it was, but there were continuing performance and dependability issues with it, and I had the engine and transmission completely rebuilt professionally in 2007.

My wife and I made the decision to move back to the USA in 2008, and I insisted we find a way to ship the old ute with us. The movers literally built a wooden rack around the car so they could fit it in the shipping container and pack furniture and such around it.

Once the car arrived in the USA, I had to go through a great deal of hassle to get it registered and insured, but I got it done. At that time, the registration folks told me this car was one of only two they have on their books for the entire USA.

I NEVER intended to sell this car.

But in 2013, we had our first son, and in 2015 we had our second.

Long story short- we never get to drive the old car anymore and we really need the money. It breaks my heart, but I have decided it's time to let it pass on to someone who can give it the love and attention it needs and deserves.

This car is so much fun to drive.

It turns a lot of heads and you make a lot of friends when you park anywhere in it!

I have a lot of personal attachment to this old car, and I really would like to see it go to a loving home.

If you want to show the car, it needs a lot of cosmetic restoration (some rust, paint, dash, shifter console, carpet, etc), but it is just fine as-is as a daily driver.

The car is insured (liability) and has antique plates and current safety inspection.

Asking $13,500

THE SPECIFICS

1970 Aussie Ford XW Falcon Ute

Was customized to look like a 1971 XY Falcon GT that is an iconic Australian muscle car.

Right-hand drive

Windsor 302 V8

C6, 3spd auto transmission, floor shift

Engine and transmission completely rebuilt professionally in 2007. Cylinders rebored, cam was slightly modified, stall converter added, GT racing radiator installed (does not overheat now).

Suspension and tires redone professionally in 2013 by Walker Tire (on North Lamar).

It has a standard Autolite 2bbl carb (I used this car as a daily driver for a long time and never drove it recklessly whatsoever).

I had a decent custom sound system installed. There's not much room, but I did what I could (and what made sense - I didn't modify the car).

No heat (could be restored), never had AC or power steering.

Driver side visor needs to be fixed (I just took it off)

Passenger-side window does not function (has new winding mechanism, but the alignment never worked right and the window kept jamming so I just fixed it shut and removed the cranking handle).

Vent-windows do not stay open (but do open)

Odometer, speedometer, fuel gauge, cigarette lighter do not function.

Hood release cable broke, so I re-engineered it to open from a pull in the front

This car is street legal, no problem to get inspected and runs on regular gas with no additives necessary. It's dependable as a daily driver.

So overall, the ute is mechanically very solid. The drive-train is pretty much new as-of 2007, and suspension and tires were redone in 2013.

I Have: rear bumper (not required, but could be re-attached), black XW 302 pinstripe set, hood pins, vinyl bed tonneau cover

BACK TO TOP
PHOTOGENIC

The ute looks great at 20 feet, especially after a bath!

Again, many of the imperfections are just not readily apparent at a bit of a distance away.

INSPECTED AND INSURED

Turn signals, lights, brake lights, wipers are all working fine.

COVER THAT BED

I have a black vinyl tonneau cover for the bed - it has a support rod that mounts easily across the middle of the bed.

OPEN SESAME

This window is not currently functional, but the winder and winding mechanism are there for the fixing. Also, the gas cap has its own key.

FLOORED

I removed the old carpet to clean and paint the floor pan. The floor has been treated with rust inhibitor and sealed with truck bed paint. The dash needs recovering and the car could use a console around the gear shift.

TAKE A SEAT

The seats aren't in perfect condition, but they are not too bad. I Armor-All them pretty regularly. The seatbelts are adjustable shoulder harnesses (not retractable).

SIMPLE PLEASURES

The odometer, speedometer and fuel gauge are not working, but could be repaired.

SUPPORTIVE

I fixed broken support springs in this seat by webbing the bottom with heavy gauge wire. I also fixed and oiled the seat slide rails so you can adjust the seats.

COCKPIT

The big steering wheel helps with the lack of power steering. The radio works great!

PANELS

The door panels should be replaceable by standard 1970 US Falcon parts.

HEADROOM

There's lots of room in this car, and the headliner is in good shape. I removed the sun visor on the driver's side because it wouldn't stay in place.

DASHED

I did say that the dash needs to be recovered, right?

A BIT RUSTY

There are a few patches of rust that need to be cut out and replaced. Also worth noting that the panels you see with screws in them in the bed allow access to the gas tank and suspension without the need of a rack. There's also an interesting drainage system in there.

NOT PERFECT

The paint is pretty rough. There are a lot of chips, cracks, peeling and oxidation.

IN MORE DETAIL

More rust. The rims are original - much sought-after "12 slots". The hubcaps were originally chrome, but I painted them to protect from further rust.

THE HOOD

The wipers are new and funtional. I have hood pins that could be installed.

SUPER ROO

The guy I bought this car from had it done up to resemble an iconic Aussie beast - the 1971 XY GTHO. These gold pinstripes are in rough shape. I have a set of black ones that are correct for this 302 XW ute (not the 351 XY GTHO stripes that are on it now).

UNDER THE HOOD

A straightforward 302 V8. The engine and transmission were rebuilt in 2007. I had a GT radiator put in to keep it from overheating (which it never does now). The carb is a 2bbl Autolite. You could put a 4bbl carb on it, but it would require a new manifold.

THE GRILL

The chrome on the grill was gone, so I went ahead and painted it matte black. The fog lights used to work, but no longer do. The bulbs look ok, so I think it's an electrical issue somewhere. They look nice though, and are not required.
